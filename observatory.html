My Role at the Observatory

I'm a proud member of the New Jersey Astronomical Association and have the incredible opportunity to work at their observatory. As an active member, I get to operate the facility's impressive 26-inch telescope and participate in various programs that bring astronomy to the community.

My responsibilities include conducting public viewing sessions, assisting with educational programs, and using the telescope for my own astrophotography projects. It's an amazing experience to share the wonders of the universe with visitors while also pursuing my passion for capturing celestial objects.

About the NJAA Observatory

The New Jersey Astronomical Association Observatory is a premier facility dedicated to astronomical research, education, and public outreach. The state-of-the-art 26-inch telescope provides exceptional views of the cosmos and serves as a hub for amateur astronomers and the public alike.

Located in a carefully selected dark sky site, the observatory offers optimal conditions for both visual observation and astrophotography. The facility is equipped with modern automation systems and is regularly used for public viewing nights, educational programs, and scientific observations by members like myself.

Equipment I Work With

Main Telescope

26-inch Newtonian Reflector

The centerpiece of the observatory is a custom-built 26-inch Newtonian reflector telescope. This large-aperture instrument provides exceptional light-gathering capability and resolution, making it ideal for observing deep space objects, planets, and lunar features. I'm fortunate to have regular access to this incredible instrument.

  • Aperture: 660mm (26")
  • Focal Length: 3300mm
  • Focal Ratio: f/5
  • Mount: Equatorial

Imaging Capabilities

Digital & Visual Observation

The telescope is equipped with both visual and digital imaging capabilities. High-quality eyepieces provide stunning visual views, while digital cameras allow for astrophotography and scientific imaging of celestial objects. I use these capabilities for both public demonstrations and my own photography projects.

  • Visual: Multiple eyepieces
  • Digital: CCD/CMOS cameras
  • Filters: Various astronomical filters
  • Focuser: Motorized focus system

Accessories & Filters

Complete Observation Suite

A comprehensive collection of filters and accessories enhances the telescope's capabilities. From narrowband filters for nebula observation to planetary filters for detailed views of our solar system neighbors. I've learned to use these effectively for different types of observations.

  • Narrowband: Ha, OIII, SII filters
  • Planetary: Color and IR filters
  • Eyepieces: Various focal lengths
  • Barlows: 2x and 3x magnification

Observatory Features

Modern Facility & Automation

The observatory features a roll-off roof design for easy access to the sky, climate control systems, and modern automation for telescope pointing and tracking. The facility is designed for both research and public education, and I help maintain and operate these systems during my shifts.

  • Roof: Roll-off design
  • Climate: Temperature controlled
  • Automation: Computerized pointing
  • Accessibility: Public viewing area

Programs I Participate In

Public Viewing Nights

I help conduct regular public viewing sessions where visitors can observe celestial objects through the 26-inch telescope. I provide guidance, answer questions about astronomy, and share my knowledge with community members who are curious about the universe.

Educational Programs

I assist with educational outreach programs for schools, scout groups, and community organizations. I help teach about astronomy, telescope operation, and the wonders of the universe, making complex concepts accessible to learners of all ages.

Member Activities

As a member, I have access to use the telescope for personal observations, astrophotography projects, and collaborative research initiatives. I also participate in member-only events and contribute to the association's mission of advancing amateur astronomy.
