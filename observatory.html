Welcome to the NJAA Observatory

The New Jersey Astronomical Association Observatory is a premier facility dedicated to astronomical research, education, and public outreach. Our state-of-the-art 26-inch telescope provides exceptional views of the cosmos and serves as a hub for amateur astronomers and the public alike.

Located in a carefully selected dark sky site, the observatory offers optimal conditions for both visual observation and astrophotography. The facility is equipped with modern automation systems and is regularly used for public viewing nights, educational programs, and scientific observations.

Equipment & Setup

Main Telescope

26-inch Newtonian Reflector

The centerpiece of our observatory is a custom-built 26-inch Newtonian reflector telescope. This large-aperture instrument provides exceptional light-gathering capability and resolution, making it ideal for observing deep space objects, planets, and lunar features.

  • Aperture: 660mm (26")
  • Focal Length: 3300mm
  • Focal Ratio: f/5
  • Mount: Equatorial

Imaging Capabilities

Digital & Visual Observation

The telescope is equipped with both visual and digital imaging capabilities. High-quality eyepieces provide stunning visual views, while digital cameras allow for astrophotography and scientific imaging of celestial objects.

  • Visual: Multiple eyepieces
  • Digital: CCD/CMOS cameras
  • Filters: Various astronomical filters
  • Focuser: Motorized focus system

Accessories & Filters

Complete Observation Suite

A comprehensive collection of filters and accessories enhances the telescope's capabilities. From narrowband filters for nebula observation to planetary filters for detailed views of our solar system neighbors.

  • Narrowband: Ha, OIII, SII filters
  • Planetary: Color and IR filters
  • Eyepieces: Various focal lengths
  • Barlows: 2x and 3x magnification

Observatory Features

Modern Facility & Automation

The observatory features a roll-off roof design for easy access to the sky, climate control systems, and modern automation for telescope pointing and tracking. The facility is designed for both research and public education.

  • Roof: Roll-off design
  • Climate: Temperature controlled
  • Automation: Computerized pointing
  • Accessibility: Public viewing area

Programs & Activities

Public Viewing Nights

Regular public viewing sessions where visitors can observe celestial objects through our 26-inch telescope. Knowledgeable members provide guidance and answer questions about astronomy.

Educational Programs

Educational outreach programs for schools, scout groups, and community organizations. Learn about astronomy, telescope operation, and the wonders of the universe.

Member Activities

Exclusive access for NJAA members to use the telescope for personal observations, astrophotography projects, and collaborative research initiatives.
